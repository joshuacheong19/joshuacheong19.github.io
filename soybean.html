01 Executive Summary

A systematic mean-reversion signal for DCE soybean meal futures built on Chinese crusher economics. Chinese crushing plants face a 2–3 week production-response lag when margins reach extremes, creating temporary dislocations between futures prices and physical economics. A naïve implementation produces statistically significant edge across 2021–2023 but collapses entirely during 2024–2025 due to structural port inventory accumulation. The failure is resolved by a regime filter on the 12-week change in Chinese port stocks, which produces a clean monotonic relationship with signal win rate and restores positive performance across the full sample — including the previously broken years.

04 Methodology
1Signal
Crush Margin Percentile
Brazilian landed-cost parity model integrating CBOT, DCE, FX, and FOB premiums across seven US-origin tariff regimes spanning 2018–2025. Margin ranked in a trailing 504-day window. Long below 15th percentile; short above 85th. Positions held 10 business days.
2Filter
Port Stock Regime Gate
12-week change in Chinese imported soybean port stocks (Bloomberg: JCIQSONT, source Shanghai JC Intelligence). When stocks build at >+200k tonnes per 12 weeks, the crusher response mechanism cannot complete within the signal horizon. Building-regime triggers are suppressed entirely.
3Risk
2× ATR Volatility Stop
2× 20-day realised volatility stop per trade — a standard managed futures convention, not an optimised parameter. No in-sample fitting on the multiplier. Stops checked daily at close. Calmar ratio of 0.6 is the strategy's principal risk consideration for production deployment.
